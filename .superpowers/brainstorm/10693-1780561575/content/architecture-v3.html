PHI Wall
Mental Health · PHIPA hard constraint
Privacy Layer
Hard rule: Mental Health records are always siloed. No consent level permits cross-program linking. Never aggregated in dashboard. Never exported to non-UW funder schemas.
Why (PHIPA)
Ontario's Personal Health Information Protection Act classifies mental health records as PHI. Consolidation without a proper PHI vault and Privacy Impact Assessment violates PHIPA. The production roadmap includes a partitioned PHI vault — the 🔒 badge and hard exclusion in the prototype demonstrate architectural maturity.

File Boundary
Why files, not live API pushes
Hard Constraint
The facts
iCARE (IRCC): bulk file upload is the IRCC-endorsed path — ~75% of records already arrive this way.

EOIS-CaMS (EO): IBM Cúram platform explicitly blocks external integration — compatibility + privacy constraints. Manual entry only.
Why this is the moat
Every CRM — Salesforce, Apricot, OCMS — hits this same wall. They all leave caseworkers re-keying into funder portals by hand. OneView generates the file. That's the entire value proposition in one sentence.

Provider-agnostic · one env var to switch
AI M&E Layer

Consent Engine
Three levels of consent
Privacy Layer
Consent hierarchy
1
Program consent — auto at enrollment. Data used for this program's funder only.
2
Cross-program consent — explicit toggle. Required to link records across programs.
3
Dashboard aggregation — implied by cross-program consent. Client appears in org-wide metrics.
Exception: Mental Health never participates regardless of consent level.
